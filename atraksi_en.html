Tourist Attractions

Karya Sari Tourism Village offers a variety of attractions combining natural beauty, traditional culture, and engaging educational activities. Each experience is designed to provide a deep impression of authentic rural life.

Rice Field Trekking

Enjoy the beauty of lush green rice fields by walking along the dikes. This activity is accompanied by an explanation of the traditional farming system and Bali's unique Subak irrigation. You can also interact directly with local farmers to gain deeper insights into their agrarian lifestyle. Perfect for tourists seeking a serene rural atmosphere.

Organic Farming Education

Visitors can participate in planting and harvesting organic vegetables directly from the garden. Additionally, you will learn how to make natural organic fertilizers. This activity is perfect for families and children who want to learn about sustainable farming. You can also take home the harvested produce as a souvenir.

Traditional Palm Sugar Making

Witness the process of making palm sugar from sap to ready-to-use powder or blocks. You can also try molding the sugar manually. This activity provides a unique experience of local traditions that are still preserved today. The process uses traditional tools such as clay stoves.

Local Handicrafts

Join workshops to make bamboo weaving, coconut leaf hats, or other traditional crafts with local artisans. Besides learning, you can also take home your creations as unique souvenirs from Karya Sari Village. This activity is perfect for tourists who want to delve into traditional arts.

Traditions & Ceremonies

These traditions reflect the spiritual and cultural values of Karya Sari Village. The procession is usually accompanied by gamelan music and traditional dances.

Educational Tourism for Schools & Communities

A visit program for students or communities that includes educational tours, traditional games, and discussions about local culture. This program is designed to provide a fun and in-depth learning experience about rural life. Participants can also engage in activities such as planting rice or making simple crafts.
